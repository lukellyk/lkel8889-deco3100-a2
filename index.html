Australia's Military Involvements and their Effects on Incoming Generations.

How does war affect their mortality and the future of their country?

By Luke Kelly | B. DesComp @ USyd '21

Introduction

Since 1990, Australia has been a participant in a multitude of conflicts overseas with many still ongoing. These wars have shaped and changed religions, races, governments, borders and even entire countries. This analysis aims to visualise the impacts of these conflicts on the future generation of these countries with regard to the Child Mortality Rates (CMR) as well as the Human Development Index (HDI) developed by the UN.
